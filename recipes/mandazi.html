Mandazi

Description

Are you ready to tantalize your taste buds with a delicious and aromatic East African delicacy? Look no further than the mouth-watering mandazi! These delectable fried bread treats are a staple in the cuisine of Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda, and for good reason. Made from a rich and flavorful dough, mandazi are often described as a cross between a donut and a muffin, with a texture that is both light and fluffy, yet satisfyingly chewy.

The aroma of cardamom and cinnamon wafts through the air as the mandazi sizzles in hot oil, creating a crispy outer shell that gives way to a warm, pillowy interior. Whether enjoyed on their own as a snack or paired with a hot cup of tea or coffee, mandazi are the perfect way to satisfy your cravings for something sweet and indulgent. So why wait? Treat yourself to the irresistible taste of mandazi today and discover the magic of East African cuisine!

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 large egg
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  • In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt.
  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the melted butter, coconut milk, and egg.
  • Gradually pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, mixing until the dough comes together.
  • Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  • Cover the dough with a clean towel and let it rest for about 30 minutes.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan or pot to 350°F/180°C.
  • Divide the dough into small pieces and shape them into triangles, circles, or any other desired shape.
  • Fry the mandazi in the hot oil until golden brown on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  • Drain the mandazi on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  • Serve warm with tea or coffee, and enjoy!
